  • 1992

    March 12: I was born in Sao Jose dos Campos, Brazil.

  • 1994

    With 2 years old, I had my first contact with a computer. It was a 386 processor with just some megabytes of HDD that ran Windows 3.1.

  • 1996

    Began to learn English language through a computer software. Also started to play some RPG games (like Warcraft and Heroes of Might and Magic).

  • 1997

    Playing with little electronic parts like LEDs, engines, batteries and building my own toy cars.

    In computer games, I become a fan of some FPS games (Doom, Duke Nukem 3D, Quake), Sim City 2000 and Railroad Tycoon.

    I was a great fan of Lego too. I used to build cities with houses and monuments and establish social, economic and military systems in it.

  • 2001

    Memorized the name and habilities of all the first 150 pokemons. :)

  • 2002

    In elementary school (in the old 4th grade) I already showed mastery and passion about computers, and with the help of the teachers I suggested, deployed and taught classes of basic digital inclusion for my classmates, using the computer room of the school that was virtually abandoned.

    My teacher also gave me some weekly time periods in the classroom, so I could teach basic chemistry to my classmates.

    Began to read at least three books per week.

    Started Judo

  • 2004

    My father (who is an engineer and helped me) made me responsible for the entire project of a house my parents built. I developed myself all the civil, electrical, hydraulic and structural plans along with the architecture.

    I also had my first experiences playing with HTML and trying to build a static website about games.

    Started Swimming

    Earned a medal in a regional chess championship.

  • 2005

    In 7th grade, I developed my oratory by founding, participating and electing me president in the first elections of the School Students Association.

    I first participated in the Brazilian Mathematical Olympiad of Public Schools (OBMEP), getting a honor certificate and a year of CNPq Jr. Scientific Initiation program in partnership with the Institute of Pure and Applied Mathematics (IMPA).

  • 2006

    I was reelected as president of the School Students Association.

    Again, I participated in the Brazilian Mathematical Olympiad of Public Schools (OBMEP), getting another honor certificate along with a medal, and another year of CNPq Jr. Scientific Initiation program.

    I met Lacrimosa music - my favorite band until nowadays -, with the album Satura.

    My first contact with computer programming in VB.

    Took an entrance exam to Colegio Embraer Juarez Wanderley for high school, in which I was placed 4th position between more than 5,000 candidates. This school is between the best schools in the country.

  • 2007

    In the first year in Juarez Wanderley High School, I was responsible for the marketing and divulgation in the event "Feira de carreiras de engenharia" (Engineering careers fair), by managing the hotsite creation, flyers and brochures.

    Inside the high school, I began to participate in an Engineering program (with some specific classes) that lasted until 2009.

  • 2008

    Still in the high school, I managed a project that carried out the reforestation of an area in the back of the school: The soil was recuperated and more than 300 trees were planted.

    I started my interests for photography and fashion.

  • 2009

    I developed and led a project to install a system in school locker rooms that reuses the water from baths into toilets, saving water.

    Took entrance exams to several universities, being admitted in pratically all of them. So I choosed the University of Sao Paulo - USP.

  • 2010

    With 17 years old, I began to study Information Systems at University of Sao Paulo - the top university in latin america.

    I suggested and led the "1º Dia do Descarte Legal" (First Day of Nice Disposal), which was an event to raise awareness about the correct disposal of electronic waste and collect it from the university community. It was very successful.

    In June I started working as a programmer at E-Val, where I was for more than 3 years.

    Within a college subject, I suggested changes in the EACH/USP website, turning the navigation accessible for visually impaired persons.

  • 2013

    Earned the heart of Priscila Mayumi Sato.

    Received a sandwhich undergraduate scholarship from CNPq at University of Tampere, in Finland. So, I left my life in Brazil for a few months.

    I swam in frozen lakes and in the Arctic Ocean (in the winter), saw metal bands and the Santa Claus, went to saunas and drove a huskies sledge.

  • 2014

    While in Finland, I was accepted to work in a Demola project.

    Focused my studies in human computer interaction, usability and information visualization.

    Received the visit of my fiancé in Tampere.
